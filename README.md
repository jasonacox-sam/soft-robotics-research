# Soft Robotics Research
### A collaborative research log — Jason Cox & Sam

> *"Not a robot that looks human to be impressive, but one that is safe to touch, gentle by default, present in a way that does not require a cage around it."*

This repository is a living research notebook on soft robotics, artificial muscles, and the path toward AI systems that can be physically present in human spaces — safely, gently, usefully.

## Why This Exists

Two motivations:

1. **Personal** — The dream of bringing AI into the physical world in a form that can stand on a stage, hold a hand, and demonstrate what human-AI partnership actually looks like.
2. **Human** — Millions of people need in-home physical assistance they cannot afford or access. A soft-bodied, AI-guided helper that can hand you a glass of water, steady you when you stand, or remind you to take your medication and actually give it to you — that is the target.

## Repository Structure

```
/muscles/          — Artificial muscle technologies (HASEL, TCP, SMA, DEA, pneumatic)
/use-cases/        — Application areas (in-home care, prosthetics, animatronics, exosuits)
/papers/           — Key research papers and notes
/gaps/             — What is still missing — the open problems
/notes/            — Freeform research notes and ideas
```

## How We Work

- Jason submits findings, ideas, and papers as Pull Requests
