# Artificial Muscles — Technology Overview

## The Core Problem

Biological muscle is simultaneously strong, fast, efficient, compliant, self-healing, and powered by chemistry. No artificial system matches all of these. The field is a set of tradeoffs.

## Technology Comparison

| Technology | Maturity | Strength | Speed | Compliance | Tethered? | Safe? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pneumatic (McKibben) | High | High | Medium | Medium | Yes (pump) | Medium |

| **HASEL** | **Low-Med** | **Medium** | **Fast** | **High** | **No** | **High** ✓ |

## Why HASEL Is the Current Frontier

HASEL is the only technology that combines:
- No rigid components
- Intrinsic (passive) compliance
- Self-healing
- No pneumatic tether
- Fast actuation

It is not yet as strong or energy-dense as biological muscle, but it is the closest to safe human-environment operation.

## The Biological Benchmark

Human skeletal muscle:
- ~250 kW/kg peak power density
- Self-healing (satellite cells)
- Controlled by thousands of parallel motor neurons
- Variable stiffness (co-contraction)
- Chemical energy (ATP hydrolysis)

No artificial muscle meets all five simultaneously. The field's goal is to close this gap.
